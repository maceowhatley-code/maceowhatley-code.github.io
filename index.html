What We're Building

I'm building a studio for documenting what's real.

Not a platform. Not extraction. Co-creation.

The people I want to work with show up and document their lives on their own terms. You bring your camera, your perspective, yourself. I bring the structure, the distribution, the community. We build the archive together.

This is my experiment in co-creation. No top-down editorial. No algorithm chasing. Just people who care, making something real.

The Centerpiece

The Weekly Video Journalism Bounty.

This is the one thing I'm asking everyone to do. Every week, We Them Media drops a journalism prompt: a question, a theme, a story worth finding. You go out, capture it on video, and share the link. Best submission wins $100.

That's the floor. One video a week, on the prompt. Everything else — your personal work, your channel, going live, posting whatever feels right — that's just you having fun. We amplify all of it. But the bounty is what keeps us moving together.

The minimum

  • One video per week on the weekly prompt
  • Upload to YouTube and share the link with us
  • Best submission wins $100 every single week

Everything else? Have fun. Post what you want, when you want. We're here for all of it.

Why an Archive

We're the local newspaper on the global level.

Most of what gets called "news" comes from people who aren't actually on the ground. I've seen it. Journalists parachute in. Algorithms surface what's trending. Platforms push what performs. Nobody's asking the person actually living it.

That's you. You're not a subject. You're not a source quoted in someone else's article. You are the primary source. I want to document everyday people living real lives because that's the story this moment actually needs.

You don't need credentials to tell the truth about your own life.

I believe this is one of the most momentous times in the 21st century. People need to know what we were thinking. What mattered to us. What we believed was true. Not what a network decided was worth covering what we actually lived.
